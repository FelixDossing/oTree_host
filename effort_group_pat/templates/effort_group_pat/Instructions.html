Introduction

Thank you for participating in our experiment!

In the following experiment, you will have the chance to earn money through effort in a simple task, which we call the slider task. Apart from working in the slider task, you will make choices, which affect both yourself and other participants. There are {{ Constants.num_rounds }} rounds in this experiment and you will have the chance to work on the slider task for {{ Constants.worktime_minutes }} minutes each round.

Please read the following instructions carefully and make sure that you understand everything before you proceed. Similar instructions are supplied in paper on your desk. If something is unclear, please raise your hand to receive help from one of the lab assistants.

The slider task

This experiment revolves around the slider task, where you can earn points by moving sliders. The point of the slider task is to move sliders from 0 to 50. Sliders are initially set at 0 and moved to 50 by clicking and dragging with the mouse. You will earn 1 point for each slider you place at 50. You can see what the slider task looks like in the picture below.


In the picture above, the participant has moved the first two sliders to 50 and has therefore earned 2 points. The participant has only moved the third slider to 46 and it does therefore not earn the participant any points yet.

Each slider is called a “task” and moving a slider from 0 to 50 is called “completing a task”. There are 48 tasks on the slider task screen. However, this does not mean that you can only complete 48 tasks per round. When all of the sliders have been set to 50, the sliders will simply reset to 0 and you will thus be able to continue completing tasks and earn more points.

After reading these instructions, you will get to try the task for {{ constants.trial_minutes }} minutes in a trial. The points that you earn in the trial will not be added to your total payoff. The point of the trail is simply to allow you to become acquainted with the slider task.

Surfing the web

As mentioned above, you will have the opportunity to work for {{ Constants.worktime_minutes }} minutes on the slider task in {{ Constants.num_rounds }} rounds. It is not possible to continue in the experiment before the time has passed. The slider task screen may however feature a button with the words “Surf the web” on it. If this is the case, then you may choose to click this button and browse the internet instead of earning points through the slider task. Completing tasks earns money but completing tasks for {{ Constants.worktime_minutes }} minutes may be boring. The “Surf the web”-button offers an opportunity to substitute the task for something more entertaining. You can see what the slider task looks like when the button is present in the picture below.


If you decide to press the “Surf the web”-button, then the slider task will close down and a new screen will open up, where you can use the internet. This means that you will not be able to complete any more tasks in that round, if you choose to press the button. The points that you have earned before pressing the button will still count.

Navigating the internet

If you press the “Surf the web” button, a new screen will open up, where you can spend the remaining time browsing online. Once the time runs out, this screen will close down automatically. The screen opens up on Google.com and you should use google to access the sites that you want. Therefore, if you want to visit youtube.com then you type “youtube” into the google search bar and access the site through the link. If you need to go forward or backward between sites, you can use the following commands:

Backward: ALT + left arrow key
Forward: ALT + right arrow key

Do not exit full screen mode or try to access sites through the URL.

Decision screens

Each round will feature a number of decision screens followed by the slider task. On the decision screens you will make choices that affect whether the “Surf the web”-button will be present on your own screen as well as on the screen of other participants. You make eight choices concerning your own slider task-screen. These are named 1A, 1B, 1C, 1D, 2A, 2B, 2C, 2D. Each of these choices is implemented with an equal probability, which means there is an equal probability that each choice will have consequences for you. It is not necessary to think too much about this – it is simply in your interest to answer in accordance with your preferences/choose the options that you prefer.

Each participant in the experiment will be given an anonymous ID such as “Player 5”. You will be randomly partnered with another participant in each round. Some of the choices that you make may affect your partner and some of the choices that your partner makes, may affect you. You will not interact with any of the other participants for more than one round.

Points and money

After the trial round, the experiment will consist of {{ Constants.num_rounds }} rounds where real money can be earned. Each completed task earns you one point and points are converted into money according to the following conversion:

1 point = {{ Constants.point_conversion }} DKK

This means that if you complete 1000 tasks throughout the {{ Constants.num_rounds }} rounds, then you will earn 200 DKK from this experiment.

Ready to begin?

If you at any point during the experiment have questions, please raise your hand and a lab assistant will be there to assist you.

When you are confident that you understand the instructions, press the next-button and continue to the slider task trial, where you can get to know the slider task.
