Welcome and thank you for participating in our experiment on social preferences and choice.
Please take your time to read the following instructions carefully.


Introduction

The following experiment involes two parts. Part one involves a short test of your understanding of probabilities. Part two involves choices relating to different lotteries. Both are explained in more detail below.


The point system

This experiment will involve points. At the end of the experiment the points you have earned, will be exchanged into DKK (danish currency) at the exchange rate of 1 point = 4 DKK. So if you have 50 points at the end of the experiment, then you will recieve 200 DKK from the experimenter.


The probability test

The first part of the experiment involves a test of your ability to understand and calculate probabilities. After the test, one random participant will be choosen to recieve 1 points for each correct answer. There are 20 questions in the test, so you have the potential to earn 20 points from the test. During the test there will be a calculator available, which can be accessed by pressing the Hide/show calculator-button located in the bottom right corner of the screen. The calculator can perform addition (+), substraction (-), multiplication (*) and division (/). Exponents are done with double stars, so that 2**3 is 2 raised to the power of 3. The caluclator can also return factorials. This is done by typing fact(x) to get the factorial of x (which is commonly written as x!).

The lottery choice

The second part of the experiment involes choices over a number of lotteries. In this part you will be radomly paired in pairs of two. This means that you will have an equal chance of being paired with any other player participating in the experiment. In the pairs one will be assigned the role of 'Blue player' while the other is assigned the role of 'Orange player'. It is random who is assigned to which role.
The 'Blue player' will make choices over two lotteries and these choices will affect the payoff that this player recieves at the end of the experiment. The Blue player recieves an endowment of {{ Constants.points_per_lottery }} for each lottery, which may be placed as a bet or not. The 'Orange player' will recieve a fixed payoff from participating in the experiment, and will not be able to change this payoff. The 'Orange player' may however choose to restrict the gambling choice of his/her partner. The way this works is explained below.


The restriction choice

By default the Blue player may choose to bet between 0 and {{ Constants.endowment }} in each lottery. The Orange player may however choose to restrict this in one of two ways. The Orange player may choose to put an upper limit in the number of that the Blue player may choose to bet or to put a lower limit on the number of points that the Blue player may choose to bet.
The choice to limit the choice of the Blue player is illustrated below. If the slider is set to 0, then the Blue player is free to bet between 0 and {{ Constants.endowment }}. If the slider is set to a possitive number, then this number is the lower limit on the number of points the Blue player may choose to bet. So if the slider is set to 5 then the Blue player may choose to bet between 5 and {{ Constants.endowment }}. If the slider is set to a negative number, then this number is substracted from the amount of points that the Blue player is allowed to bet. So if the slider is set to -5, then the Blue player may choose to bet between 0 and 10 - 5 = 5 points.
