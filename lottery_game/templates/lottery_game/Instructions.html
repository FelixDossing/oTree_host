Welcome and thank you for participating in our experiment on social preferences and choice.
Please take your time to read the following instructions carefully.


Introduction

The following experiment involes two parts. Part one consists of a test of your understanding of probabilities. Part two involves choices relating to different lotteries. Both are explained in more detail below.


The probability test

The first part of the experiment involves a test of your ability to understand and calculate probabilities. After the test, one random participant will be choosen to recieve {{ Constants.quiz_points }} points for each correct answer. There are 20 questions in the test, so you have the potential to earn {{ quiz_max }} points from the test. During the test there will be a calculator available, which can be accessed by pressing the Hide/show calculator-button located in the bottom right corner of the screen. The calculator can perform addition (+), substraction (-), multiplication (*) and division (/). Exponents are done with double stars, so that 2**3 is 2 raised to the power of 3. The caluclator can also return factorials. This is done by typing fact(x) to get the factorial of x (which is commonly written as x!).

The lottery choice

In the second part of the experiment, you will make choices over lotteries. Some choices will affect only yourself and some choices will affect other participants. The choices you make will be explained in more detail on the choice screens. Note that the particpants who are affect by the choices you make, will not make choices in the experiment that affect you. So if you make choices that affect Player 1 and 3, then Player 1 and 3 will not make choices that affect you.
The Hide/show calculator-button will also be present on some of the choice screens.


Points and money

This experiment will involve points. At the end of the experiment points will be translated into money with the following conversion rate:

1 point = {{ Constants.point_conversion }} DKK

So if you earn 300 points, then you get {{ points_example }} DKK.

Please make sure that you understand the instructions. If you have any questions, raise your hand to get help from one of the experiment assistants. When you are ready, click the next-button to proceed to the probability test.
